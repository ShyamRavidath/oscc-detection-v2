## Design Context

### Users
Academic researchers, clinicians, potential biotech collaborators, and science communicators who land on the site to evaluate the OralSense project. They are scientifically literate, visually discerning, and time-constrained. Use case: rapidly assess project credibility, explore the proteomics data, understand the LFA assay mechanism, and reach out for collaboration.

### Brand Personality
Precise · Urgent · Restrained. The site is a research portfolio for an OSCC (oral squamous cell carcinoma) early-detection project. The tone should feel like a high-end biotech preprint — serious, data-forward, with a quiet confidence that the work speaks for itself.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark science instrument: deep near-black backgrounds (#07090f), teal as the primary data accent (#00e5c8), signal red (#ff2d55) for T/C lines and positive results — mirroring the actual LFA strip colours. Space Grotesk for all UI copy, DM Mono for numerical/data values, Instrument Serif for the single hero italic. Anti-reference: no purple/blue gradient AI aesthetic, no glassmorphism decoration, no spinning loaders or particle effects unrelated to the assay simulation.

### Design Principles
1. **Data is the design.** Every visual element should either encode information or create negative space that lets information breathe. Nothing decorative.
2. **Laboratory precision.** Spacing, alignment, and typography should feel measured and exact — not rigid, but intentional.
3. **Signal over noise.** Teal marks content the user should engage with; red marks findings and results. All other UI chrome recedes.
4. **Earned complexity.** The scroll animation, the interactive model, and the Q&A are progressively revealed — not dumped on the user at once.
5. **Credibility through restraint.** The site convinces through clear data and tight copy, not marketing language.
